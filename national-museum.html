About the National Museum

The National Museum of the Philippines is the official repository of the country's natural and cultural heritage. Housed in several neoclassical buildings near Rizal Park, the museum complex features extensive collections spanning archaeology, anthropology, fine arts, and natural history.

The main National Museum of Fine Arts building was originally designed as the Old Legislative Building in 1926 and is itself a National Cultural Treasure.

Museum Complex

  • National Museum of Fine Arts - Houses iconic works like Juan Luna's Spoliarium
  • National Museum of Anthropology - Showcases Philippine ethnography and archaeology
  • National Museum of Natural History - Features biodiversity and geological exhibits
  • National Planetarium - Astronomy and space science presentations

Must-See Exhibits

  • Juan Luna's "Spoliarium" (1884) - The most famous Philippine painting
  • The San Diego Archaeological Collection - Artifacts from a 17th-century shipwreck
  • The Tabon Man Exhibit - Earliest human remains found in the Philippines
  • Contemporary Philippine Art Galleries
  • The Tree of Life - Stunning architectural centerpiece of Natural History museum
