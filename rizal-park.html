Rizal Park

Manila's iconic urban park and tribute to the Philippines' national hero, Dr. Jose Rizal

About Rizal Park

Also known as Luneta Park, this 58-hectare historical urban park is where the Philippine national hero, Dr. Jose Rizal, was executed by the Spanish colonial government on December 30, 1896. The park serves as a tribute to his martyrdom and features numerous monuments, gardens, and open spaces.

Today, Rizal Park is a favorite leisure spot for both locals and tourists, hosting cultural events, concerts, and national celebrations.

Key Attractions

  • Rizal Monument - The iconic bronze and granite memorial marking the execution site
  • Musical Dancing Fountain - Evening light and water shows set to music
  • National Museum Complex - Located adjacent to the park
  • Chinese and Japanese Gardens - Beautifully landscaped thematic gardens
  • Orchidarium and Butterfly Pavilion - Showcasing Philippine flora and fauna
  • KM 0 Marker - The point from which all distances in the Philippines are measured

Visitor Information

Location: Along Roxas Boulevard, Manila

Opening Hours: 5:00 AM to 9:00 PM daily

Entrance Fees: Free (some attractions may charge separately)

Best Time to Visit: Early morning or late afternoon; fountain shows at 6-7 PM

Transportation: Accessible via LRT-1 (United Nations Station), jeepneys, taxis

Tips for Your Visit

  • Wear comfortable shoes as the park covers a large area
  • Bring water and sun protection during daytime visits
  • Visit the Rizal execution site at dawn for a solemn experience
  • Check the schedule for flag-raising ceremonies (usually Sundays)
  • Combine your visit with nearby Intramuros and National Museum
  • Be mindful of your belongings in crowded areas
